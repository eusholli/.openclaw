# AT&T Sales Intelligence Report: 2026 Strategic Priorities

**Date:** March 6, 2026
**Confidentiality:** For internal sales reconnaissance use.

## 🎯 Executive Summary
AT&T is in the middle of a massive pivot toward **"Connected AI"** and **Open RAN** infrastructure, backed by a $14B multi-year commitment. Their 2026 goal is to have **70% of wireless traffic** flowing over open-capable platforms. Simultaneously, they are aggressively expanding their **Industrial Edge** footprint to capture Industry 4.0 revenue.

---

## 🚀 Key Sales Entry Points

### 1. AI-Native Network Software (The "Connected AI" Strategy)
AT&T is moving away from proprietary "black box" hardware. They need AI-native software that optimizes network performance at the edge.
*   **Focus Area:** Link Adaptation, interference cancellation, and traffic steering.
*   **Hardware Alignment:** Solutions must be compatible with **Intel Xeon 6 SoC** and Cloud RAN architectures.
*   **The Angle:** "Our AI reduces your L1/L2 processing overhead on standard hardware."

### 2. Industry 4.0 & Industrial Edge
At MWC 2026, AT&T emphasized bundling fiber connectivity with AI workloads for enterprise.
*   **Focus Area:** Computer vision for manufacturing, predictive maintenance for IoT, and autonomous logistics.
*   **The Angle:** "We provide the 'intelligence' layer for the private 5G/Fiber bundles you are selling to manufacturing and logistics hubs."

### 3. Convergence Automation
AT&T is merging its fiber and 5G build-outs into a single "Strategic Convergence" roadmap to reduce CAPEX.
*   **Focus Area:** Automated site design, digital twins for network planning, and permitting automation.
*   **The Angle:** "We can accelerate your 5G/Fiber site build-out by X% through automated planning and permitting."
